Galaxy S25 Ultra vs iPhone 16 Pro Max: Battery Life Test

ProZiphy April 11, 2025 • 5 min read

Battery life is one of the most critical aspects of modern smartphones, especially for flagship devices like the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra and Apple iPhone 16 Pro Max. In this detailed comparison, we’ll explore how these two premium devices perform under real-world conditions, analyzing their endurance, charging capabilities, and overall usability. Whether you’re a power user or someone who values long-lasting performance, this article will help you determine which phone is the better choice.

Why Battery Life Matters

A smartphone’s battery life directly impacts its usability and convenience. Whether you’re streaming videos, navigating maps, gaming, or simply browsing social media, a reliable battery ensures uninterrupted usage throughout the day. Both Samsung and Apple have made significant strides in optimizing battery performance for their flagship models, but how do they stack up against each other in 2025?

The Test Setup: Real-World Conditions

To ensure unbiased results, comprehensive battery tests were conducted under standardized conditions:

  • Both devices were brand new with batteries at 100% health.
  • Settings such as brightness, resolution, and connectivity were configured identically.
  • The test simulated typical daily activities including GPS navigation, video playback, gaming, photography, and wireless hotspot usage.

These scenarios reflect high-demand tasks that significantly impact battery consumption, providing a realistic evaluation of each phone’s endurance.

Battery Specifications

The Galaxy S25 Ultra and iPhone 16 Pro Max come equipped with impressive hardware to support their battery performance:

Feature Galaxy S25 Ultra iPhone 16 Pro Max
Battery Capacity 5,000mAh 4,685mAh
Video Playback Time Up to 31 hours Up to 33 hours
Charging Speed (Wired) 45W (USB-C) 30W (USB-C)

Reverse Wireless Charging 4.5W N/A

The Results: Galaxy S25 Ultra vs iPhone 16 Pro Max

The battery drain test revealed notable differences between the two devices:

  • Galaxy S25 Ultra: Lasted an impressive 13 hours and 30 minutes under heavy usage scenarios. It excelled in tasks like wireless hotspot usage and social media browsing due to its efficient power management.
  • iPhone 16 Pro Max: Managed a respectable 11 hours of endurance but drained faster during high-demand tasks such as gaming and video streaming.

The Galaxy S25 Ultra emerged as the clear winner in terms of overall battery longevity.

Charging Capabilities: Speed and Convenience

The charging technologies on both devices are designed to minimize downtime:

  • S25 Ultra: Supports fast wired charging at up to 45W and wireless charging at 15W. Its Wireless PowerShare feature allows users to charge other devices like earbuds or smartwatches.
  • iPhone 16 Pro Max: Offers wired charging at up to 30W and MagSafe wireless charging at an impressive 25W. While it lacks reverse wireless charging, its Qi2 compatibility ensures broad accessory support.

If fast charging is a priority for you, both phones deliver excellent performance with slight variations in speed and flexibility.

The Role of Display Technology in Battery Consumption

The display is one of the most power-hungry components of any smartphone:

  • S25 Ultra: Features a LTPO AMOLED panel with adaptive refresh rates ranging from 1Hz to 120Hz. Its anti-reflective coating enhances outdoor visibility while reducing energy consumption.
  • iPhone 16 Pro Max: Utilizes a Super Retina XDR OLED display with similar refresh rate capabilities but tends to maintain higher brightness levels even in darker environments, leading to faster battery depletion.

The adaptive brightness control on the Galaxy S25 Ultra gives it an edge in energy efficiency during prolonged use.

User Experience: How Battery Life Impacts Daily Usage

The differences in battery performance translate into distinct user experiences:

  • S25 Ultra: Ideal for power users who rely heavily on their smartphones for multitasking, gaming, and outdoor activities. Its longer battery life ensures fewer interruptions throughout the day.
  • iPhone 16 Pro Max: Better suited for users who prioritize video playback quality and seamless integration with Apple’s ecosystem over extended endurance.

The Verdict: Which Smartphone Has Better Battery Life?

Your choice between the Galaxy S25 Ultra and iPhone 16 Pro Max depends on your specific needs:

  • If you require a smartphone that lasts longer under heavy usage scenarios and offers versatile charging options like reverse wireless charging, the Galaxy S25 Ultra is the superior choice.
  • If you value faster wireless charging speeds and exceptional video playback quality within Apple’s ecosystem, the iPhone 16 Pro Max may be more appealing.

No matter which device you choose, both deliver premium experiences tailored to different user preferences.
