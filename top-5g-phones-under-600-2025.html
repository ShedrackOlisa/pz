Top 5G Phones Under $600 in 2025

ProZiphy April 11, 2025 • 5 min read

The year 2025 has brought a plethora of affordable yet feature-packed 5G smartphones to the market. With advancements in technology, you no longer need to spend over $1,000 to enjoy fast connectivity, powerful processors, and excellent cameras. This guide explores the top 5G phones under $600, comparing their features, performance, and value across leading brands like Apple, Samsung, Google, and OnePlus.

Why Choose a Budget 5G Smartphone?

Budget smartphones have evolved significantly over the years. In 2025, manufacturers have successfully integrated flagship-level features into mid-range devices. Whether it's high-refresh-rate displays, long-lasting batteries, or AI-powered cameras, these phones deliver excellent value without breaking the bank.

For users seeking reliable performance and future-proof connectivity at an affordable price, these top 5G phones are worth considering.

Best 5G Phones Under $600

Here are the standout options for budget-conscious buyers:

  • iPhone 16e: Apple’s entry-level iPhone offers seamless integration with iOS and impressive performance.
  • Samsung Galaxy S24 FE: A flagship Android experience at a mid-range price.
  • Google Pixel 8a: Known for its exceptional camera and software support.
  • OnePlus 13R: A powerhouse for performance and fast charging enthusiasts.
  • Samsung Galaxy A35 5G: A budget-friendly option with a stunning AMOLED display.

iPhone 16e

The iPhone 16e is Apple’s answer to budget-conscious buyers who want access to the iOS ecosystem without compromising on quality. Priced at $599, it offers a compact design with a bright Retina HD display that measures 6.1 inches.

Powered by the A15 Bionic chip, the iPhone 16e delivers smooth performance for multitasking and gaming. Its camera system includes a single lens capable of capturing sharp photos with natural colors. While it lacks some advanced features found in higher-end models like ProMotion or telephoto lenses, it excels in reliability and ease of use.

The battery life is sufficient for a day’s use under moderate conditions, and its integration with Apple’s ecosystem makes it an appealing choice for those already invested in services like iCloud or Apple Music.

Samsung Galaxy S24 FE

The Samsung Galaxy S24 FE (Fan Edition) is designed for users who want flagship-level features without paying premium prices. At $599, it delivers a stunning Super AMOLED display with a resolution of 2400 x 1080 pixels and a refresh rate of up to 120Hz.

This phone is powered by the Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 chipset paired with up to 8GB of RAM. It handles demanding tasks effortlessly while offering excellent gaming performance. The triple-camera setup includes a versatile main sensor that excels in both daylight and low-light conditions.

The battery capacity of 4,500mAh ensures all-day usage with support for fast charging at up to 25W. Samsung’s commitment to software updates adds further value to this device.

Google Pixel 8a

The Google Pixel series has always been synonymous with exceptional photography, and the Pixel 8a continues this legacy. Priced at $499, it features Google’s Tensor G3 chipset alongside AI-powered tools like Magic Eraser and Real Tone.

The camera system includes a single high-resolution lens capable of capturing stunning images even in challenging lighting conditions. While its design may not be as sleek as competitors due to larger bezels, its compact size (6.1 inches) makes it easy to handle.

The Pixel 8a also boasts seven years of software updates—a significant advantage for users seeking long-term reliability.

OnePlus 13R

If speed is your priority, the OnePlus 13R is an excellent choice at $499. This phone stands out for its blazing-fast charging speeds (up to 100W), allowing users to fully charge its battery in just under half an hour.

The device is powered by MediaTek Dimensity chipset paired with up to 12GB of RAM for seamless multitasking and gaming performance. Its AMOLED display measures 6.74 inches with vibrant colors and fluid animations thanks to its high refresh rate.

The camera system is decent but falls short compared to Google’s Pixel series. However, if you prioritize performance over photography, the OnePlus 13R delivers incredible value.

Samsung Galaxy A35 5G

The Samsung Galaxy A35 is one of the best options under $400 for users seeking reliable performance without frills. It features a Super AMOLED display that measures 6.6 inches with vibrant colors ideal for media consumption.

This phone is powered by MediaTek Dimensity chipset paired with sufficient RAM for everyday tasks like browsing or streaming videos. Its camera system includes multiple lenses capable of capturing decent photos but lacks advanced features found in higher-priced models.

The battery life is impressive thanks to its large capacity (5,000mAh), ensuring all-day usage even under heavy workloads.

The Verdict: Which Phone Should You Choose?

Your choice depends on your priorities:

  • If you prefer iOS: Go for the iPhone 16e for seamless integration with Apple services.
  • If you want flagship-level features: The Galaxy S24 FE delivers excellent value at $599.
  • If photography matters most: Choose the Pixel 8a for its outstanding camera capabilities.
  • If speed is key: The OnePlus 13R offers unmatched charging speeds alongside solid performance.
  • If affordability is your priority: The Galaxy A35 provides reliable features at just $399.
